# 🤖 AI2 Robotics Raises 144 Million in Series B to Scale AlphaBot to 10,000 Units

> **🤖 Robotics** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-01

---

## 📋 Summary
China-based AI2 Robotics has closed a CN1.2 billion (approximately USD 144.7 million) Series B round, pushing the company's valuation past RMB 10 billion (USD 1.4 billion). The round was led by Baidu, CRRC (China's state-owned high-speed train manufacturer), Yusys Technologies, Sentury Tire, and Guotai Haitong Securities. The company plans to scale its AlphaBot humanoid robot production from 1,000 units in 2025 to 10,000 units in 2026, with Baidu supporting AI capability development and CRRC providing manufacturing expertise.

## 💡 Why It Matters
AI2 Robotics' Series B reflects accelerating investor confidence in embodied AI commercialization, with a 10x production scale-up target and strategic partners providing both AI capability (Baidu) and manufacturing scale (CRRC). The involvement of a state-owned train manufacturer as a strategic investor signals that China's industrial sector is treating humanoid robotics as a serious near-term deployment target for heavy industry use cases. Global embodied AI investment exceeded $6 billion in the first seven months of 2026, and AI2's round adds to that momentum.

## 🔍 What Is This?
AI2 Robotics builds humanoid robots — robots with a human-like body designed to work in physical environments built for humans. The AlphaBot series is designed for general-purpose tasks. With this new funding, the company plans to manufacture ten times more robots this year than last year, backed by strategic partners including Baidu (AI software) and CRRC (industrial manufacturing).

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
The partnership structure — AI lab (Baidu) plus heavy industry manufacturer (CRRC) — is a template for humanoid robot commercialization that combines AI capability with manufacturing scale. If AI2 Robotics hits its 10,000-unit production target in 2026, it would represent one of the largest-volume humanoid robot deployments globally and establish a benchmark for the sector.
