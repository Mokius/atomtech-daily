# ⚙️ Micron Ramps HBM4 Production With 25 Billion Dollar Capex Expansion

> **⚙️ Hardware & Chips** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-01

---

## 📋 Summary
Micron Technology has confirmed that its entire 2026 high-bandwidth memory (HBM) supply is sold out under binding contracts, with HBM4 — the next generation of the memory technology critical to AI accelerators — entering production ramp in Q2 2026. The company has committed to a $25 billion capital expenditure plan for fiscal year 2026, expanding DRAM and HBM manufacturing capacity in Taiwan to meet sustained AI infrastructure demand. DRAM and NAND bit shipments are projected to grow approximately 20% in calendar 2026.

## 💡 Why It Matters
High-bandwidth memory is the performance-limiting bottleneck in AI accelerators like the H100 and MI300 series. As AI training and inference workloads scale, demand for HBM has consistently outpaced supply, creating a structural constraint on the pace of AI deployment. Micron's $25B capex and HBM4 production ramp signal that the memory industry is making the large-scale capacity bets needed to sustain the AI infrastructure buildout through 2027 and beyond.

## 🔍 What Is This?
High-bandwidth memory (HBM) is a specialized type of fast memory used in AI chips. Without enough of it, AI systems slow down — it is one of the key bottlenecks in building and running large AI models. Micron is investing $25 billion to build more factories and ramp up production of the newest HBM4 generation, trying to keep supply in step with the explosive demand driven by the global AI buildout.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
HBM4 production ramp will directly influence the AI chip roadmaps of Nvidia, AMD, Google, and other accelerator vendors, all of whom depend on Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung for memory supply. Micron's aggressive capex signals confidence that AI infrastructure spending remains robust, and provides a supply-side backstop for the next generation of AI hardware platforms.
