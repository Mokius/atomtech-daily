# 💻 OpenClaw v2026.4.27 Ships Google Meet Integration and Memory Wiki

> **💻 Developer Tools** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-01

---

## 📋 Summary
OpenClaw, the open-source personal AI assistant that surged from 9,000 to over 210,000 GitHub stars in 2026, released version 2026.4.27 with native Google Meet integration, a people-aware memory wiki with full provenance views, expanded multi-provider support including NVIDIA and AWS Bedrock Opus 4.7, and new locale support for Persian, Dutch, Vietnamese, Italian, Arabic, and Thai. The project now ships updates approximately every two days.

## 💡 Why It Matters
OpenClaw has become the fastest-growing open-source AI project in GitHub history, capturing developer mindshare by offering a self-hosted alternative to commercial AI assistants that works across OS, messaging platforms, and now video conferencing. The memory wiki feature addresses a long-standing limitation of AI assistants — contextual continuity across sessions — while keeping all data on the user's own infrastructure. The rapid release cadence signals a healthy, high-velocity contributor community.

## 🔍 What Is This?
OpenClaw is a free, open-source AI assistant that runs on your own computer or server rather than in a cloud service. It connects to the messaging apps, video calls, and tools you already use, answers questions, remembers past conversations in a private memory system, and can be configured to use whichever AI model you prefer — all without your data leaving your control.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
As OpenClaw matures, expect it to emerge as enterprise infrastructure for organizations that require air-gapped or data-sovereign AI assistance. The growing provider support (NVIDIA, Bedrock, OpenAI-compatible APIs) positions it as a universal abstraction layer over the fragmented AI model ecosystem.
