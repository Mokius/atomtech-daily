# 🤖 Google Releases Gemma 4 Multimodal Open Models Under Apache 2.0

> **🤖 Artificial Intelligence** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-01

---

## 📋 Summary
Google released Gemma 4 on April 2, 2026, a family of open models built on the same research as Gemini 3, now available under a commercially permissive Apache 2.0 license. The lineup spans four sizes — effective 2B, 4B, 26B MoE, and 31B Dense — with native support for images, video, audio, and over 140 languages. Context windows reach up to 256K tokens, and the 31B Dense variant reached third place on the Chatbot Arena text leaderboard.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Gemma 4 makes frontier multimodal capabilities freely available to any developer or organization, eliminating the API cost and data-privacy tradeoffs that come with hosted model access. By running fully offline on devices ranging from smartphones to Raspberry Pi, the models unlock edge AI applications previously reserved for specialized hardware. The Apache 2.0 license removes commercial restrictions that hampered enterprise adoption of earlier open models.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Gemma 4 is a family of open-source AI models that can process text, images, video, and audio all at once. Unlike AI assistants that require an internet connection, Gemma 4 runs entirely on your own device — from a phone to a server — so your data never has to leave your hardware.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Gemma 4 is likely to accelerate on-device AI integration across mobile apps, edge computing products, and enterprise systems where data residency is a concern. Expect a wave of Gemma 4-powered applications targeting healthcare, finance, and government sectors where cloud AI has faced regulatory friction.
