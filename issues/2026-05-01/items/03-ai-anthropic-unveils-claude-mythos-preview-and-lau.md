# 🤖 Anthropic Unveils Claude Mythos Preview and Launches Project Glasswing

> **🤖 Artificial Intelligence** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-01

---

## 📋 Summary
Anthropic has released Claude Mythos Preview, its most advanced model to date, with strikingly powerful cybersecurity capabilities including the ability to find and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in real codebases and reverse-engineer exploits on closed-source software. The model has already identified thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities — including a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD — in its first weeks of operation. In response, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, partnering with AWS, Apple, Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, Cisco, and others to use Mythos defensively to secure critical software.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Claude Mythos Preview represents a qualitative shift in AI security capabilities, moving from AI-assisted vulnerability research to near-autonomous exploit discovery at scale. Project Glasswing is a direct acknowledgment that offensive capability requires a defensive counterpart, and its roster of participants — spanning cloud providers, hardware manufacturers, and cybersecurity firms — signals industry recognition that the threat surface has fundamentally expanded. Anthropic has chosen not to make Mythos generally available, reflecting novel safety tradeoffs at this capability level.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Claude Mythos is an AI model that can read software code and discover hidden security flaws — bugs that attackers could exploit to break into systems. Project Glasswing is a program where top tech companies are using this same AI to find and fix those flaws before attackers can take advantage of them. It is a race between offense and defense, with AI on both sides.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Mythos-class models signal an era of AI-driven continuous security auditing, where organizations may rely on AI to discover vulnerabilities faster than human researchers. The restricted access model may become a template for future dual-use AI capabilities: controlled deployment with vetted partners before any broader release.

## 💼 LinkedIn Post
Anthropic just disclosed something remarkable: Claude Mythos Preview found thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities in its first weeks of operation — including a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD.

The model can autonomously find and exploit vulnerabilities in real open-source codebases, and reverse-engineer exploits from closed-source software. Anthropic describes it as a step-change in capability, not just an incremental improvement.

Their response was Project Glasswing — a controlled program giving AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, Microsoft, and Nvidia early access to use Mythos defensively to secure critical software infrastructure.

Anthropic is not making Mythos generally available. This is a significant decision, and the reasoning is straightforward: the offensive capability is too powerful to release without guardrails in place.

This is what responsible deployment of dual-use AI looks like — acknowledging the capability, pairing it with a defensive program, and controlling access until the ecosystem can handle it.
