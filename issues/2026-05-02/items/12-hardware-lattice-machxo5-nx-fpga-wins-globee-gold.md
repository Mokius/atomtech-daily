# 🔧 Lattice MachXO5-NX FPGA Wins Globee Gold Award for First CNSA 2.0 Post-Quantum Cryptography

> **🔧 Hardware & Chips** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-02

---

## 📋 Summary
Lattice Semiconductor's MachXO5-NX TDQ FPGA family received a Gold Award in the "Best New Security Solution" category at the 2026 Globee Cybersecurity Awards. The recognition centers on the chip family being the industry's first FPGA to deliver CNSA 2.0 (Commercial National Security Algorithm Suite 2.0) compliant post-quantum cryptography in hardware. CNSA 2.0 is the NSA's updated cryptographic standard designed to resist attacks from quantum computers.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Quantum computers capable of breaking current asymmetric cryptography (RSA, ECC) are projected to emerge within the next decade. CNSA 2.0 compliance represents proactive hardware-level preparation for the post-quantum era. Implementing these algorithms in dedicated FPGA hardware rather than software enables the performance levels required for real-time secure communications in defense, government, and critical infrastructure deployments.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Post-quantum cryptography refers to encryption methods that can resist attacks from future quantum computers, which could break today's widely used security algorithms. The MachXO5-NX is a programmable chip that implements these new quantum-resistant algorithms directly in hardware — faster and more power-efficient than software solutions — and is the first of its kind to meet the NSA's updated security standard.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
CNSA 2.0 compliance will increasingly become a procurement requirement for defense and government technology contracts. Lattice's early hardware implementation gives it a competitive advantage as agencies begin mandating post-quantum cryptography standards, with NIST's PQC standardization driving adoption across commercial markets as well.
