# 🤖 NVIDIA Releases Newton 1.0 Open Source Physics Engine for Robot Dexterous Manipulation

> **🤖 Robotics** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-09

---

## 📋 Summary
NVIDIA announced the general availability of Newton 1.0, an open source physics simulation engine designed specifically for dexterous robot manipulation. Newton 1.0 provides fast and reliable physics simulation with accurate collision detection, realistic object contact modeling, and stable simulation of complex physical systems. The engine is designed to serve as the foundation for training robot manipulation policies that transfer reliably to real hardware.

## 💡 Why It Matters
The "sim-to-real" gap — the challenge of training robots in simulation and having that training transfer reliably to real-world operation — is one of the biggest obstacles in robotics research. Newton 1.0's focus on accurate collision and contact physics directly addresses this problem. An open source physics engine from NVIDIA gives the robotics research community a high-performance, maintained simulation foundation without the fragmentation of dozens of competing simulation environments.

## 🔍 What Is This?
A physics simulation engine models how physical objects interact — how they fall, collide, deform, and move. For robotics training, simulation is essential because training robots entirely on real hardware is extremely slow and expensive. Newton 1.0 is specifically tuned for dexterous manipulation — the kind of fine-grained hand and finger movements required for tasks like assembling parts, handling delicate objects, or operating tools.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Newton 1.0's open source release will likely become the default physics simulation environment for robot manipulation research, similar to how PyTorch became the default ML framework. Expect rapid ecosystem growth — tools, pre-trained models, and research benchmarks built on Newton 1.0 — over the next 12 months.
