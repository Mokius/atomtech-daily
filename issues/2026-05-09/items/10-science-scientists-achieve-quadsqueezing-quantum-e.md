# 🔬 Scientists Achieve First-Ever Quadsqueezing — A Fourth-Order Quantum Effect

> **🔬 Science** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-09

---

## 📋 Summary
Scientists have achieved the first experimental demonstration of quadsqueezing — an elusive fourth-order quantum effect that provides a new, powerful method for controlling quantum systems. The breakthrough extends beyond conventional quantum squeezing (which reduces uncertainty in one property at the cost of another) by achieving simultaneous control at the fourth-order statistical level of quantum fluctuations. The result was published in a peer-reviewed journal following the experimental confirmation.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Quadsqueezing opens new possibilities for quantum sensing, quantum communication, and quantum computing by enabling more precise control over quantum states than was previously achievable. Higher-order squeezing is particularly valuable for quantum error correction — a key bottleneck in building practical quantum computers — and for ultra-precise measurement instruments. This first experimental confirmation validates years of theoretical predictions.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Quantum squeezing is a technique that reduces uncertainty in one measurable property of a quantum system (like position) at the expense of increased uncertainty in another (like momentum). Quadsqueezing achieves this at the fourth-order statistical level — a more complex and precise form of quantum control analogous to the difference between controlling the average of a process versus controlling its higher-order variability.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Fourth-order quantum control could accelerate progress in quantum sensing applications — including ultra-precise gravitational wave detectors, quantum clocks, and quantum-enhanced imaging systems — before its implications fully reach quantum computing hardware. The techniques developed for quadsqueezing will likely be adopted by quantum hardware labs within two to three years.
