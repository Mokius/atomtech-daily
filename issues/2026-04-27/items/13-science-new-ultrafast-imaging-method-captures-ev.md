# New Ultrafast Imaging Method Captures Events in Trillionths of a Second

> **🔬 Science** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-04-27

---

## 📋 Summary
Scientists unveiled a breakthrough single-shot imaging technique capable of capturing structural changes in physical and biological materials at picosecond timescales (trillionths of a second). Unlike previous stroboscopic methods requiring thousands of repeated shots to reconstruct an event, the new method captures the full event in one exposure, revealing previously invisible intermediate states. The technique has immediate applications in materials science, drug discovery, and fusion energy research.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Many critical physical and chemical processes — molecular bonding, phase transitions, protein folding — happen too fast to observe with existing imaging tools. Capturing them in a single shot rather than reconstructing them across thousands of trials makes real-time monitoring of these processes possible for the first time.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Imagine trying to photograph a bullet in flight but your camera requires you to take 10,000 photos and stitch them together. This new technique takes one photo and captures the full event perfectly — in a timeframe a million times shorter than a single camera flash.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Expect the technique to be commercialized for pharmaceutical research within 2–3 years, significantly accelerating drug discovery by enabling real-time observation of molecular interactions.
