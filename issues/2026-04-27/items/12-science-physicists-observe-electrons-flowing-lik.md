# Physicists Observe Electrons Flowing Like Frictionless Liquid in Graphene

> **🔬 Science** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-04-27

---

## 📋 Summary
A team of physicists reported that electrons in graphene were observed flowing in a nearly frictionless viscous liquid state, in apparent violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law — a foundational principle in condensed matter physics that has held for over 160 years. This phenomenon, called hydrodynamic electron flow, had been theoretically predicted but not cleanly observed at this level until now. The research used cryogenic graphene samples with extreme purity.

## 💡 Why It Matters
The Wiedemann-Franz law describes the relationship between how well a material conducts heat versus electricity. Observing a clear violation opens new theoretical territory and has practical implications for ultra-low-resistance electronics and quantum computing components.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Normally, electrons in a metal move somewhat chaotically, bumping into each other and losing energy as heat. In this experiment, electrons in graphene behaved like a smooth, coordinated fluid — losing almost no energy. This could eventually lead to electronics that waste far less power.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
This discovery could guide the development of next-generation low-power chips and superconductor-adjacent materials. Materials science groups will attempt to replicate this in other 2D materials.
