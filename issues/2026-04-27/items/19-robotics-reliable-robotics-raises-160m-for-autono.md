# Reliable Robotics Raises $160M for Autonomous Aircraft Operations

> **🦾 Robotics** · 🟢 Notable · 2026-04-27

---

## 📋 Summary
Reliable Robotics closed a $160 million funding round with investors including Nimble Partners, Eclipse Ventures, Lightspeed Venture Partners, and Coatue. The company is developing autonomous flight systems for cargo aircraft, enabling existing planes to fly with reduced or zero crew for cargo operations. The funding will accelerate FAA certification efforts and fleet expansion with commercial cargo operators.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Autonomous cargo aviation addresses a genuine labor shortage in commercial aviation while unlocking cost efficiencies for e-commerce and logistics. FAA certification is the critical gate — Reliable’s progress toward it represents a meaningful step toward commercially viable autonomous freight.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Reliable Robotics builds technology that can fly existing cargo planes autonomously — essentially an autopilot capable enough to replace the pilot entirely for freight flights. This isn’t flying cars; it’s retrofitting real commercial aircraft already in service.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
FAA certification timelines typically run 2–4 years. If Reliable Robotics achieves certification, expect major logistics carriers (FedEx, UPS, DHL) to be early customers. The $160M signals the market believes certification is achievable.
