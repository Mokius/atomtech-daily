# Google Cloud Launches Two Specialized TPU 8 Chips for the Agentic Era

> **☁ Cloud & Infrastructure** · 🔴 High · 2026-04-27

---

## 📋 Summary
At Google Cloud Next 2026, Google unveiled its eighth-generation TPU platform featuring two distinct chips: TPU 8i for inference and reinforcement learning, and TPU 8t for training. TPU 8i triples on-chip SRAM to 384 MB, increases HBM to 288 GB, doubles ICI bandwidth to 19.2 Tb/s, and reduces network diameter by over 50%. These chips are designed specifically for the demands of running large agentic AI systems at scale.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Custom silicon is the decisive competitive moat in cloud AI. Google’s TPU advantage means it can run AI workloads faster and more cheaply than competitors relying on third-party GPUs. The agentic specialization of TPU 8 signals that Google is building infrastructure around the assumption that AI agents are the dominant workload of the next generation.

## 🔍 What Is This?
TPUs are specialized chips Google designed specifically to run AI calculations — similar to how GPUs were optimized for graphics. The eighth generation is Google’s most powerful yet, designed specifically for autonomous AI agent workloads.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
TPU 8 availability will accelerate Google Cloud’s position as the preferred platform for frontier AI training and deployment. Watch for competitive responses from AWS (Trainium) and Azure (Maia) at their own developer conferences.
