# Microsoft April Patch Tuesday: 164 CVEs Including 3 Defender Zero-Days

> **🔐 Cybersecurity** · 🔴 High · 2026-04-27

---

## 📋 Summary
Microsoft’s April 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed 164 CVEs across its product suite, including 8 Critical-severity vulnerabilities. Three zero-day vulnerabilities in Microsoft Defender were found being actively exploited in the wild, with the most dangerous (BlueHammer) weaponized since at least April 10. Two of the three Defender zero-days remain unpatched as of this writing. CISA has separately added 6 known exploited vulnerabilities to its KEV catalog.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Microsoft Defender is the security product protecting millions of Windows machines including enterprise environments. Zero-days actively exploited before a patch is available represent the highest-priority threat category. Organizations running Windows should apply April patches immediately and monitor for BlueHammer indicators of compromise.

## 🔍 What Is This?
A zero-day is a security vulnerability that hackers are exploiting before the software vendor has released a fix. Patch Tuesday is Microsoft’s monthly security cycle — April’s batch of 164 patches is one of the largest in recent memory.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
The two remaining unpatched Defender zero-days require organizations to implement mitigating controls and heightened monitoring until out-of-band patches are released. Expect Microsoft to issue emergency patches within days.
