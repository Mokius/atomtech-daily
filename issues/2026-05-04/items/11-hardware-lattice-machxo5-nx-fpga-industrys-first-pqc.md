# 🔧 Lattice MachXO5-NX FPGA Becomes Industry's First With CNSA 2.0 Post-Quantum Cryptography

> **🔧 Hardware** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-04

---

## 📋 Summary
Lattice Semiconductor's MachXO5-NX TDQ FPGA family has received a Gold Award at the 2026 Globee Cybersecurity Awards for being the industry's first FPGA family to deliver CNSA 2.0-compliant post-quantum cryptography (PQC) and crypto agility. CNSA 2.0 (Commercial National Security Algorithm Suite 2.0) is the NSA's next-generation cryptographic standard designed to resist attacks from cryptographically relevant quantum computers. The MachXO5-NX integrates hardware-level PQC directly into the FPGA's secure configuration and boot processes.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Quantum computers capable of breaking current RSA and ECC encryption — the cryptographic foundation of virtually all secure communications — are increasingly considered a near-term threat rather than a distant theoretical concern. CNSA 2.0 compliance in hardware FPGAs means secure boot, configuration, and operational security can be quantum-resistant at the silicon level — without software patches or protocol upgrades. This is particularly critical for aerospace, defense, and critical infrastructure applications where hardware must remain secure for decades.

## 🔍 What Is This?
FPGAs (Field-Programmable Gate Arrays) are chips that can be configured after manufacturing — unlike regular processors that are fixed in function. They're used in critical applications ranging from defense systems to industrial control. Post-quantum cryptography uses math problems that even quantum computers can't solve quickly, protecting sensitive systems against the coming generation of quantum threats. Having this protection built directly into the hardware chip, rather than layered on top in software, is significantly more robust.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
As the NSA-mandated transition to CNSA 2.0 compliance deadline for national security systems approaches in the late 2020s, hardware vendors across the semiconductor industry will face pressure to deliver CNSA 2.0-certified silicon. Lattice's early mover advantage here positions MachXO5-NX as a reference design for quantum-resistant hardware root of trust implementations.
