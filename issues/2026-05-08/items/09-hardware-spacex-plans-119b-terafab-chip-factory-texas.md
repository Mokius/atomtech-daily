# ⚙️ SpaceX Plans $119 Billion Terafab Chip Factory in Texas for AI and Autonomous Systems

> **⚙️ Hardware & Semiconductors** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-08

---

## 📋 Summary
SpaceX is considering spending up to $119 billion on "Terafab," a next-generation vertically integrated semiconductor manufacturing and advanced computing fabrication facility in Grimes County, Texas. The initial phase may reach $55 billion. Intel has already signed on as a manufacturing partner. The facility aims to produce chips for AI servers, SpaceX satellites, an envisioned space-based data center, and Tesla's autonomous vehicles and robots.

## 💡 Why It Matters
If completed, Terafab would be the single largest private semiconductor investment in US history, dwarfing the $40B TSMC Arizona investment. It signals that xAI's compute ambitions have outgrown the supply chain — and that Musk is betting on vertical integration (owning chip design through manufacturing) as a competitive moat for AI and autonomous systems.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Terafab is a massive planned chip factory that would design and manufacture semiconductors from scratch — rather than outsourcing chip production to TSMC or Samsung like most US tech companies currently do. It's part of a broader strategy to control the entire AI hardware stack in-house.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
A vertically integrated US semiconductor facility at Terafab's scale would reduce dependence on TSMC and reshape AI chip supply chains — with major implications for national security policy, NVIDIA's data center GPU business, and the global AI infrastructure race.
