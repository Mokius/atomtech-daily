# 🚀 Era Raises $11M to Build Software Platform for AI-Powered Gadgets and Consumer Devices

> **🚀 Startups & Venture** · 🟢 Notable · 2026-05-08

---

## 📋 Summary
Era (also known as Era Computer) has raised $11 million in total funding, including a $9 million seed round led by Abstract Ventures and BoxGroup with participation from Collaborative Fund and Mozilla Ventures. The startup is building a software platform that acts as an operating system layer for AI-powered consumer gadgets — providing a unified software foundation for the emerging category of dedicated AI hardware devices. The round was disclosed in late April 2026.

## 💡 Why It Matters
The hardware AI gadget space — which includes devices like the Rabbit R1, Humane AI Pin, and emerging form factors — has struggled with software fragmentation and poor user experience. Era's bet is that a shared software platform, similar to how Android unified Android smartphones, could enable better AI gadgets by abstracting the hardware layer and providing a common development target for app builders.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Era is trying to build the "Android" for AI gadgets — a software layer that any AI hardware maker could build on, so developers write AI applications once and they run on many different devices. Think of it as the plumbing that makes AI gadgets actually useful.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
If AI gadget hardware finds its product-market fit in 2026-2027 (following several high-profile early misfires), Era's platform could become critical infrastructure for the category — similar to how RTOS and Android established the software stack for smart speakers and smartphones respectively.

## 💼 LinkedIn Post
Era raised a $9M seed round in April 2026 ($11M total) to build what might be best described as the Android for AI gadgets.

The pitch: the emerging AI hardware category — dedicated AI devices beyond smartphones — needs a shared software foundation to enable developers to build once and deploy across many form factors. Era provides that platform layer.

The investor thesis is interesting: Abstract Ventures, BoxGroup, Collaborative Fund, and Mozilla Ventures are betting that AI gadgets will find their footing after the initial wave of misfires, and that the software platform will be more durable than any individual hardware vendor.

For founders and hardware makers: if you're building a dedicated AI device and want to avoid rebuilding the OS layer from scratch, Era is worth a conversation. For developers: a platform play in an emerging hardware category means early movers can establish the developer ecosystem that creates platform lock-in.

The category still has to prove itself, but the software problem Era is solving is real regardless of which specific form factor wins.
