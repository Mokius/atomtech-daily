# 🤖 Anthropic Releases Claude Opus 4.7 with Coding and Visual Reasoning Improvements

> **🤖 Artificial Intelligence** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-08

---

## 📋 Summary
Anthropic launched Claude Opus 4.7 this week, delivering notable improvements over Opus 4.6 in advanced software engineering tasks, especially on the most complex benchmarks. The model gains substantially better vision capabilities — processing images at higher resolution — and produces higher-quality professional outputs including documents, slides, and interfaces. Opus 4.7 is priced identically to Opus 4.6 at $5 per million input tokens and $25 per million output tokens.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Claude Opus 4.7 narrows — or according to VentureBeat analysts — briefly recaptures the lead over competing frontier models for general-purpose tasks. The parity pricing signals that Anthropic is competing on capability rather than cost-cutting, which should accelerate adoption in enterprise coding and research workflows.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Opus 4.7 is Anthropic's most capable Claude model to date, designed for tasks requiring deep reasoning, multi-step planning, and precise instruction-following over long contexts. It also ships with a new Cyber Verification Program allowing security professionals to unlock additional cybersecurity use cases.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
With Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google all releasing major model iterations within weeks of each other, the frontier AI race in 2026 is producing measurable capability gains on a near-monthly cadence — transforming enterprise software development pipelines in real time.
