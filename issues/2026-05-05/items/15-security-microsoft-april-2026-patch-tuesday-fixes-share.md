# 🔐 Microsoft April 2026 Patch Tuesday Fixes SharePoint Zero-Day and 160 Vulnerabilities

> **🔐 Security & Infrastructure** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-05

---

## 📋 Summary
Microsoft's April 2026 Patch Tuesday addressed 160 security vulnerabilities, including an actively exploited zero-day in SharePoint. The SharePoint vulnerability allowed remote attackers to execute arbitrary code on vulnerable servers without authentication under certain configurations. The 160-vulnerability patch batch is one of the largest single Patch Tuesday releases in recent years, spanning Windows, Office, Azure, and numerous other Microsoft products. Organizations are urged to prioritize deployment of the patches.

## 💡 Why It Matters
SharePoint is deployed in hundreds of thousands of enterprise environments globally as a core document management and collaboration platform. An unauthenticated RCE zero-day in SharePoint that has been actively exploited represents a serious threat vector — attackers can gain a foothold in enterprise networks through a commonly trusted internal tool. The scale of 160 total vulnerabilities also indicates broad attack surface exposure requiring prioritized triage.

## 🔍 What Is This?
SharePoint is Microsoft's enterprise platform for sharing files and collaborating — used by millions of businesses. The zero-day allowed attackers to take control of SharePoint servers without needing a login. Microsoft's April Patch Tuesday is the monthly batch of security fixes it releases; this month's was unusually large at 160 fixes.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Organizations running SharePoint on-premises are at highest risk and should prioritize patching immediately. SharePoint Online customers with fully managed Microsoft tenants received automatic updates. The volume of April patches also suggests a growing backlog of vulnerabilities being discovered across Microsoft's extensive product surface.
