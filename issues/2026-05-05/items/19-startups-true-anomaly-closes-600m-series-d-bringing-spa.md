# 🚀 True Anomaly Closes $600M Series D Bringing Space Security Startup to $1.1B Total Funding

> **🚀 Startups, Robotics & More** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-05

---

## 📋 Summary
True Anomaly, a space security startup, has closed a $600 million Series D funding round led by Riot Ventures and Eclipse, bringing its total funding to approximately $1.1 billion. True Anomaly builds spacecraft and software systems designed for space domain awareness and on-orbit security — tracking objects and potential threats in Earth orbit. The raise reflects growing government and commercial demand for space situational awareness capabilities as orbital congestion increases.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Space is becoming a contested environment as the number of satellites, debris fields, and dual-use spacecraft increases dramatically. True Anomaly's technology — detecting, tracking, and characterizing objects in orbit — addresses a critical national security and commercial infrastructure need. A $600M Series D at this scale signals that space security is attracting serious capital as it moves from a niche government domain to a broader commercial and defense market.

## 🔍 What Is This?
True Anomaly builds spacecraft and software to watch what's happening in Earth orbit — tracking satellites, debris, and objects that could threaten critical space infrastructure. Think of it as air traffic control and security monitoring for space. With thousands of satellites now in orbit and more launching every month, this kind of situational awareness is increasingly important.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
As the commercial satellite economy expands and geopolitical competition in space intensifies, demand for space domain awareness and on-orbit security services will grow substantially. True Anomaly's $1.1B in total funding positions it to become the category-defining company in this emerging sector.
