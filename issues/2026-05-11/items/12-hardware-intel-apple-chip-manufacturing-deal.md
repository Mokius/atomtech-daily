# 🖥️ Intel and Apple Reach Preliminary Agreement for Intel to Manufacture Apple Chips

> **🖥️ Hardware** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-11

---

## 📋 Summary
Intel has reportedly reached a preliminary agreement with Apple to manufacture some of Apple's chips, according to a Wall Street Journal report cited by Channel IAM on May 9, 2026. The two companies are said to have been in detailed discussions for over a year, with a formal agreement reached in recent months. Following the news, Intel shares jumped sharply while Apple stock also saw a modest rise. The deal would represent a significant new revenue stream for Intel's foundry business and a meaningful diversification of Apple's chip manufacturing supply chain away from sole reliance on TSMC.

## 💡 Why It Matters
This deal could be transformative for Intel's foundry strategy — having Apple as a customer would be a massive credibility boost and volume commitment for Intel's manufacturing capabilities. For Apple, reducing dependence on TSMC (particularly as US-Taiwan geopolitical tensions remain a concern) represents an important supply chain resilience measure. The deal signals that Intel's process technology has advanced to a point where it can compete for Apple's demanding specifications.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Apple designs its own chips (like the M-series and A-series processors) but pays other companies to manufacture them. Currently, almost all Apple chips are made by TSMC in Taiwan. Intel, which traditionally designs and makes its own chips, has been trying to build a business making chips for other companies too — and landing Apple would be its biggest win yet.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
If finalized, this deal would accelerate Intel's foundry credibility and could attract additional major fabless customers. It would also strengthen the US domestic semiconductor manufacturing ecosystem as part of ongoing CHIPS Act-driven investment. TSMC's position as Apple's sole manufacturer of flagship chips would be challenged for the first time.
