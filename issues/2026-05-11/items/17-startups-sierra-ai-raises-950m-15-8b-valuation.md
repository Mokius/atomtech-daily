# 🚀 Sierra AI Raises $950M at $15.8B Valuation for Enterprise AI Agent Platform

> **🚀 Startups** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-11

---

## 📋 Summary
Sierra, the enterprise AI agent startup co-founded by Bret Taylor (former Salesforce co-CEO and OpenAI board chair), raised $950 million in a new funding round at a $15.8 billion post-money valuation. The round was led by Tiger Global and GV (Google Ventures), nearly doubling Sierra's previous funding total. Sierra builds AI agents for enterprise customer experience, enabling businesses to automate complex customer service interactions with AI agents that can handle nuanced conversations and integrate with existing enterprise systems.

## 💡 Why It Matters
A $15.8 billion valuation for a company focused specifically on enterprise AI agents signals that investors see this as a massive market opportunity — and that Sierra has demonstrated the product quality and enterprise traction to command premium pricing. Bret Taylor's involvement (having led both Salesforce and served on OpenAI's board) gives Sierra unique credibility and strategic access. This round further validates enterprise AI agents as one of the most significant near-term AI deployment categories.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Sierra builds AI-powered customer service agents for large companies — software that can handle complex customer conversations that previously required human agents. At a $15.8 billion valuation, investors are betting this type of enterprise AI application will become as essential as the CRM systems and contact center platforms it is displacing.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Sierra's massive raise will accelerate hiring and product development, likely pushing expansion into new enterprise workflow categories beyond customer service. The round will also intensify competition from Salesforce, ServiceNow, and other enterprise software giants who are rapidly building their own AI agent capabilities.
