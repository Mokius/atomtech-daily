# 🔬 Super-Earth GJ 887 d Found in Habitable Zone Just 10.7 Light-Years Away

> **🔬 Science** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-04-30

---

## 📋 Summary
Astronomers announced the discovery of GJ 887 d, a super-Earth exoplanet orbiting within the habitable zone of a nearby red dwarf star called Gliese 887, located just 10.7 light-years from Earth. This makes it the second-nearest known exoplanet in a stellar habitable zone. The planet's proximity makes it an exceptional candidate for follow-up atmospheric characterization by next-generation telescopes.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Habitable zone exoplanets at this distance are extraordinarily rare targets — close enough that next-generation space telescopes could feasibly analyze their atmospheres for biosignature gases like oxygen or methane within the next decade. GJ 887 d's proximity makes it one of the highest-priority targets in the search for extraterrestrial life.

## 🔍 What Is This?
A super-Earth is a planet larger than Earth but smaller than Neptune — potentially rocky, potentially with liquid water. The "habitable zone" is the range of orbital distances where a planet could have liquid water on its surface, given the right atmosphere. At 10.7 light-years away, GJ 887 d is in our cosmic backyard.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
The James Webb Space Telescope and upcoming missions like the Habitable Worlds Observatory could attempt to characterize GJ 887 d's atmosphere within the next decade. A detection of oxygen or methane at such proximity would be among the most consequential scientific discoveries in human history.
