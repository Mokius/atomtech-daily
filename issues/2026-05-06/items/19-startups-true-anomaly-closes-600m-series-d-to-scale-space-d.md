# 🚀 True Anomaly Closes $600M Series D to Scale Space Defense Drone Fleet

> **🚀 Startups & Funding** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-06

---

## 📋 Summary
True Anomaly, a Colorado-based aerospace startup, closed a $600 million Series D led by Riot Ventures and Eclipse, bringing total funding to approximately $1.1 billion. The company builds small, flexible space drones and interceptors designed for missile defense and "space superiority" missions. True Anomaly is sponsored by U.S. defense agencies and targets a rapidly growing market for space-based defense capabilities as orbital competition intensifies between the U.S., China, and Russia.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Space is increasingly a contested domain, with multiple nations developing capabilities to disable or destroy adversary satellites and space assets. True Anomaly's raise reflects substantial government conviction that commercial aerospace startups can deliver space defense capabilities faster and more cost-effectively than traditional defense primes. The $1.1 billion total funding signals this is a priority program area.

## 🔍 What Is This?
True Anomaly builds small robotic spacecraft designed for military use — inspecting, tracking, or intercepting other objects in orbit. They raised $600 million because the U.S. military wants more flexible, rapidly deployable space defense systems that commercial startups can build faster than traditional defense contractors.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
True Anomaly's Series D will fund fleet expansion and mission deployment. As orbital conflict scenarios become a serious defense planning area, the company will compete with Starfish Space, Rocket Lab, and prime contractors for U.S. Space Force contracts. International allied defense sales could follow.
