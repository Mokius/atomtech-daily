# 🔬 AI Tool RAVEN Discovers Over 100 Hidden Exoplanets in NASA TESS Mission Data

> **🔬 Science & Discovery** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-05-06

---

## 📋 Summary
Astronomers have deployed a powerful new AI tool called RAVEN to analyze data from NASA's TESS (Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite) mission, discovering over 100 previously hidden planets including rare and extreme worlds. RAVEN uses machine learning to identify subtle planetary transit signals in light curve data that traditional analysis methods had missed, dramatically expanding the known exoplanet catalog.

## 💡 Why It Matters
The discovery of over 100 new exoplanets from existing TESS data demonstrates the power of AI to extract scientific value from datasets that have already been analyzed by conventional methods. This approach — applying new AI tools to existing scientific archives — could yield similar breakthroughs across astronomy, genomics, and climate science datasets. Several of the newly discovered planets are described as "rare and extreme worlds" that could inform planetary formation theory.

## 🔍 What Is This?
TESS is a NASA satellite that watches stars for tiny dips in brightness caused by planets passing in front of them. RAVEN is an AI system that combs through all the star brightness data and finds patterns too subtle for traditional software to catch — revealing over 100 planets that were hiding in the data all along.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
RAVEN is expected to be applied to the full TESS archive, which could yield hundreds more planet discoveries. Similar AI-driven reanalysis tools are being developed for data from the James Webb Space Telescope and the Vera Rubin Observatory.
