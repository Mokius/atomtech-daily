# 🖥️ TSMC Unveils A13 and N2U Process Nodes for 2029 Alongside New Arizona Packaging Facility

> **🖥️ Hardware** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-04-29

---

## 📋 Summary
TSMC unveiled its A13 and N2U process node roadmap targeting 2029 production, and announced the opening of a new advanced packaging facility in Arizona as part of its US manufacturing expansion. The company cautioned that high demand and hardware costs could extend chip shortages beyond 2027 despite the capacity investments. AI-related hardware revenue is on track to reach $700 billion by Q4 2026.

## 💡 Why It Matters
TSMC's process node roadmap sets the pace for the entire semiconductor industry — every major chip designer from Apple to Nvidia depends on TSMC's fabrication capabilities. The A13 node represents a continued push toward smaller, denser chips that deliver more AI compute per watt. The Arizona facility represents a geopolitical diversification of chip production away from Taiwan, reducing supply chain concentration risk.

## 🔍 What Is This?
TSMC is the world's largest semiconductor foundry — the company that physically manufactures most of the world's advanced chips. Process nodes like A13 and N2U refer to how small the transistors on those chips are: smaller transistors mean more computing power per chip at lower energy cost.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
The A13 and N2U nodes will power the AI chips of 2029 — meaning today's roadmap announcement will determine what GPUs, TPUs, and inference accelerators are available when agentic AI becomes fully mainstream. Continued chip demand from AI will sustain TSMC's pricing power through the decade.
