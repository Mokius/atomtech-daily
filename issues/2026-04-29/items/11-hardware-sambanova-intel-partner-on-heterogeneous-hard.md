# 🖥️ SambaNova and Intel Partner on Heterogeneous Hardware Solution for Agentic AI Inference

> **🖥️ Hardware** · 🟡 Medium · 2026-04-29

---

## 📋 Summary
SambaNova announced the next phase of its collaboration with Intel, delivering a heterogeneous hardware solution specifically designed for demanding agentic AI inference workloads. The architecture combines Nvidia GPUs for prefill, Intel Xeon 6 processors as host and action CPUs, and SambaNova's specialized RDU (Reconfigurable Dataflow Unit) chips for decode. The solution is scheduled for availability to enterprises, cloud providers, and sovereign AI programs in H2 2026.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Agentic AI inference has fundamentally different hardware requirements than standard LLM inference — it requires sustained performance across long multi-step reasoning chains, rapid context switching, and concurrent tool execution. A purpose-built heterogeneous architecture that divides prefill, decode, and orchestration across specialized chips could deliver significant efficiency gains for production agent workloads compared to GPU-only approaches.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Most AI inference today runs on one type of chip (GPUs). This partnership creates a system using three different types of chips, each optimized for a specific part of the AI thinking process — like using specialized machines on an assembly line instead of one general-purpose robot to do everything.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
If this heterogeneous architecture delivers on its performance claims, it could reshape how enterprises build inference infrastructure for agentic AI systems — shifting procurement conversations away from pure GPU counts toward specialized chip combinations. The H2 2026 availability makes this relevant for 2027 AI infrastructure planning cycles.

## 💼 LinkedIn Post
SambaNova and Intel announced a heterogeneous hardware architecture specifically designed for the inference demands of agentic AI — a meaningful step toward purpose-built infrastructure for the next wave of AI applications.

The architecture divides responsibilities across three chip types: Nvidia GPUs handle the prefill phase (initial context processing), Intel Xeon 6 processors manage the host and orchestration layer, and SambaNova's RDU chips handle the decode phase (token generation).

This matters because agentic AI has different inference characteristics than single-shot LLM responses. Agent workloads involve long chains of reasoning, repeated context reads, and concurrent tool calls — patterns that GPU-only infrastructure handles inefficiently.

The joint solution targets enterprises, cloud providers, and sovereign AI programs, with H2 2026 availability. Organizations building out 2027 AI inference infrastructure should evaluate whether heterogeneous architectures offer a better TCO for their specific workload profiles.
