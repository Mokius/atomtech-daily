# 🔐 Palo Alto Networks Patches Critical PAN-OS Zero-Day CVE-2026-0300 Exploited in the Wild

> **🔐 Security** · 🔴 High · 2026-05-07

---

## 📋 Summary
Palo Alto Networks has confirmed a critical zero-day vulnerability (CVE-2026-0300) in PAN-OS, the operating system running its widely deployed firewall and VPN appliances. The vulnerability affects the Captive Portal service on PA and VM series firewalls and has been actively exploited in the wild. Initial patches are expected to be released by May 13, with interim workaround guidance published for administrators unable to wait.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Palo Alto Networks firewalls are deployed at the network perimeter of many of the world's largest enterprises and government agencies. A zero-day in PAN-OS with active exploitation means attackers have had an unknown window of opportunity to compromise high-value networks that organizations assumed were protected.

## 🔍 What Is This?
A zero-day vulnerability is a security flaw that the software vendor doesn't know about — giving attackers a head start on exploitation. CVE-2026-0300 is a flaw in Palo Alto's firewall software, the kind of product organizations rely on to protect their entire network. If it's exploited, attackers could potentially bypass the organization's perimeter defenses.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
Active exploitation of perimeter security products continues to be a primary attack vector for advanced threat actors. Organizations should expect increased scrutiny of firewall and VPN appliance security practices, and more mandatory patching timelines from regulators.
