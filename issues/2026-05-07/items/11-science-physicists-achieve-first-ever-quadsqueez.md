# ⚛️ Physicists Achieve First-Ever Demonstration of Quadsqueezing, a Fourth-Order Quantum Effect

> **🔬 Science** · 🟢 Notable · 2026-05-07

---

## 📋 Summary
Physicists have demonstrated quadsqueezing for the first time — a fourth-order quantum effect in which quantum noise is suppressed beyond the limits achievable with conventional squeezing techniques. The achievement unlocks a new class of quantum states with enhanced stability against noise and errors. Researchers say this could significantly advance the performance of quantum sensors and potentially improve error correction in quantum computers.

## 💡 Why It Matters
Quantum noise and errors are among the most significant obstacles to building practical quantum computers and sensors. Quadsqueezing offers a new tool for suppressing these errors at a fundamental level, potentially unlocking performance improvements in quantum technologies that conventional methods cannot achieve.

## 🔍 What Is This?
Quantum systems are incredibly sensitive to noise and errors — even tiny disturbances can ruin a quantum calculation. Scientists have developed techniques to 'squeeze' this noise, pushing it into less important parts of the system. Quadsqueezing is a new, more powerful version of this technique that suppresses noise even further than was previously thought possible.

## 🔭 Looking Ahead
If quadsqueezed quantum states can be reliably produced and maintained, they could enable quantum sensors with unprecedented precision and quantum computers that require fewer error-correction resources — accelerating the timeline to quantum advantage.
